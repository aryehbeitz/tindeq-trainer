Every climber who trains with a Tindeq Progressor knows this problem: you're hanging from a board with burning fingers, trying to glance at your phone on the shelf below to see your force numbers.

It's awkward. It's distracting. And sometimes the phone falls.

So I built a Garmin watch app that connects directly to the Tindeq Progressor via Bluetooth — no phone needed during training.

What it does:
→ Real-time force display on your wrist while hanging
→ 4 training modes: Free Pull, Repeaters, Endurance, Peak Test
→ Haptic alerts for hang/rest transitions (you feel it, no need to look)
→ Target force with vibration warning if you drop below
→ Session history saved on the watch
→ FIT file recording — sessions sync to Garmin Connect
→ Save and load named workout programs

This is the first Tindeq Progressor app on the Garmin Connect IQ platform.

Built it in a single session using Claude Code as my copilot — from reading the BLE protocol docs to a working app on my FR955. The whole thing is open source (GPL-3.0):

🔗 github.com/aryehbeitz/tindeq-trainer

If you climb and train with a Tindeq + Garmin watch — try it out and let me know what you think.
